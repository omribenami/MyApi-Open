## Lawsuit Protection Strategy

### 1. Liability Cap ($100 max)
- Prevents large judgments
- Makes suing economically unfeasible
- Applies to all claim types
- Survives contract termination

### 2. Class Action Waiver
- Prevents group actions
- Forces individual suits
- Each user needs separate lawyer
- Dramatically increases cost per user
- Result: Few users will sue individually

### 3. Arbitration Clause
- No access to courts (for many states)
- Faster resolution (6-12 months vs. 2-3 years)
- Lower costs (single arbitrator vs. jury trial)
- Private process (confidential)
- Limited appeals
- Result: Cheaper to defend

### 4. AS-IS Disclaimer
- No product liability
- No warranty claims
- No expectation-setting liability
- Result: Cannot be sued for "defects"

### 5. Indemnification Clause
- User pays Company's defense costs
- Deters frivolous suits
- Users fund opposing counsel
- Result: Users won't sue if they have to pay our legal team

### 6. Third-Party Exclusion
- Cannot sue over OAuth failures
- Cannot sue over AWS downtime
- Cannot sue over Cloudflare issues
- Result: 70% of problems are not MyApi's fault

## What YOU are NOT Protected From

### ❌ Not Protected Against
1. **Criminal Activity**
   - Using service to commit fraud
   - Using service for illegal purposes
   - Money laundering
   - Result: Criminal prosecution

2. **Gross Negligence/Willful Misconduct**
   - Intentionally causing harm
   - Reckless security (some jurisdictions may override caps)
   - Result: Caps may not apply

3. **Data Privacy Violations (Some)**
   - GDPR violations may have statutory damages (up to 4% revenue)
   - CCPA violations ($100-750 per user)
   - Intentional violations have higher penalties
   - Result: Privacy caps often don't apply to statutory damages

4. **IP Infringement**
   - If MyApi infringes user's IP
   - If service contains pirated code
   - Result: Liability may not be capped

5. **Personal Injury**
   - If MyApi's service causes physical harm
   - Unlikely but possible
   - Result: Liability not capped

6. **Fraud**
   - If you explicitly promise something then don't deliver
   - If you lie about features/security
   - Result: Fraud claims override disclaimers
